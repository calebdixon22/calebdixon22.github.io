The Threat Next Door: Countering Chinese Urban Gray-Zone Intelligence Operations Adjacent to Philippine EDCA Sites

RAND School of Public Policy · Master's Capstone · 2026

Original, unclassified intelligence and policy assessment of People's Republic of China (PRC) gray-zone intelligence operations targeting U.S. rotational forces at Philippine Enhanced Defense Cooperation Agreement (EDCA) sites. Delivered a comparative two-base assessment of Basa Air Base (Pampanga) and Antonio Bautista Air Base (Palawan), mapping commercial-cover, HUMINT, ISR, and insider-threat vectors against the Hague Centre for Strategic Studies' taxonomy of Chinese coercion. Produced under the Mutual Defense Treaty–Visiting Forces Agreement–EDCA alliance framework and benchmarked against Japan's Important Land Survey and Regulation Act and the Republic of Korea's military base protection regime.

  • TradecraftAll-source OSINT · structured geolocation protocol (EXIF analysis, satellite imagery, two-stream corroboration) · comparative case-study design · alliance and legal analysis · open-source GEOINT
  • Source familiesPrimary legal instruments (MDT, VFA, EDCA, RA 7227, RA 12312, EO 74) · think-tank and academic analysis · investigative and local journalism · geospatial imagery
  • Threat focusPRC intelligence collection · commercial-cover surveillance (RTK satellite mapping, POGO-linked networks) · HUMINT and insider threat · supply-chain and contractor risk at U.S. agreed locations
  • DeliverableNine bilateral policy recommendations across legal reform, military-to-military cooperation, and U.S.-specific lines of effort, each paired with implementation-risk and mitigation analysis
  • AudienceU.S. defense and intelligence policymakers · USINDOPACOM and allied counterparts · alliance managers and counterintelligence practitioners
